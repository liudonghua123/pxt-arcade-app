Customize Your Racer

Introduction

In this tutorial, we’ll customize your running game to make it even more special.

2. Review

Take a look at the code that’s already in your workspace.

You should see the blocks that set up your peach and the blocks that make you jump.

There should also be three overlap containers:

  • one that ends the game when you hit an hurdle
  • one that ends the game when you hit branches
  • and one that ends the game when you reach the end of the track.

Is something missing?
Click “Replace my code” below to replace the code in your workspace with recommended code.

3. Keep Rollin’

Let’s add a block to animate your peach.

  • :mouse pointer: Click the Advanced label in the toolbox to reveal the ||animation:Animation|| category.

  • :sync: Drag the
    ||animation: animate [mySprite]||
    block into the end of the ||loops:on start|| container.

4. Peachy

Choose your peach animation.

  • :paint brush: Click the empty grey square to choose the peach animation from My Assets.

  • :mouse pointer: When the peach is selected, the interval should fill automatically, but you can replace it with a larger number if you want the animation to run more slowly.

  • :mouse pointer: Toggle loop to <ON> to keep the animation playing over and over for the whole game.


let mySprite = sprites.create(assets.image`peach`, SpriteKind.Player)

mySprite.vx = 100

Remember to play your game again each time you make a change.
