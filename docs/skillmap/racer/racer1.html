Ready, Set, Go!

Introduction

Let‚Äôs make a racer game!

In this tutorial, you‚Äôll add a truck to your game and make it move through a cave.

2. Add the Truck

Before we can add a truck, we need to create a sprite.

~hint What‚Äôs a sprite? üí°

In Arcade, each character or image that does something is called a SPRITE.

Sprites have properties that you can use and change ‚Äì things like scale, position, and lifespan are all properties of sprites.

Our truck will be a sprite, too.

hint~

  • :paper plane: From the ||sprites:Sprites|| category, drag
    ||variables(sprites):set [truck] to sprite [ ] of kind [Player]||
    to the end of the empty
    ||loops(noclick):on start||
    container in the workspace.

~hint What does that mean? ü§∑üèΩ

In this tutorial, we‚Äôll use formatted text to help you know which blocks you need. For example, when we ask you to look for:
||variables(sprites):set [truck] to sprite [ ] of kind [Player]||
we‚Äôre talking about this block:

Need help finding the category? Click on the highlighted text and we‚Äôll open the drawer for you.

hint~

3. Add the Truck

Choose your truck

  • :paint brush: To choose a truck, click the empty box inside
    ||variables(noclick):set [truck] to sprite [ ] of kind [Player]||
    then switch to My Assets and choose a truck.

    üí° This image shows the truck2 sprite, but you can use whichever truck you want.

Step 4

  • :binoculars: Take a look at the game window.

Do you see the truck hovering in the cave?

5. Add Gravity

To get your game going, it needs gravity.

~hint How do I add gravity? üì•

For gravity, we‚Äôll add acceleration to ‚Äúpull down‚Äù on the sprite. This is done by setting the sprite‚Äôs ay (acceleration in the direction of ‚Äòy‚Äô).

hint~

  • :paper plane: From the ||sprites:Sprites|| category, drag
    ||sprites:set [truck] [ay (acceleration y) to [500]||
    to the end of the
    ||loops(noclick):on start|| container.
let truck: Sprite = null
truck.ay = 500
let truck = sprites.create(assets.image`truck1`, SpriteKind.Player)
// @highlight
truck.ay = 500

Step 6

  • :binoculars: Look at the game window again.

Did the truck fall to the ground?

7. Time to Drive

In this game, your truck needs to start rolling as soon as the game starts.

~hint How do I make it move? üõª

To make the truck move to the right, you need to set its vx (velocity in the direction of ‚Äòx‚Äô) to a positive number.

hint~

  • :paper plane: From ||sprites:Sprites||, drag
    ||sprites:set [truck] [vx (velocity x)] to [100]||
    to the end of the
    ||loops(onclick):on start|| container.
let truck: Sprite = null
truck.vx = 100
let truck = sprites.create(assets.image`truck1`, SpriteKind.Player)
truck.ay = 500
// @highlight
truck.vx = 100

Step 8

  • :binoculars: Look at the game window again and watch your truck mosey.

9. Follow that Sprite

Did you notice that your truck drives off-screen?

Let‚Äôs make the game ‚Äúcamera‚Äù follow the sprite so you can always see it.

  • :tree: To keep the truck in sight, go to ||scene:Scene|| and drag
    ||scene:camera follow sprite [truck]||
    to the end of the
    ||loops(noclick):on start|| container.
let truck = sprites.create(assets.image`truck1`, SpriteKind.Player)
truck.ay = 500
truck.vx = 100
// @highlight
scene.cameraFollowSprite(truck)
